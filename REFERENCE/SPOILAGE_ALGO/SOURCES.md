# Food Safety Parameters - Sources and References

## Overview
All food parameters in the spoilage algorithm are based on USDA Food Safety guidelines, FDA recommendations, and peer-reviewed food science research.

---

## DAIRY PRODUCTS

### Milk
- **Shelf Life (7 days)**: USDA FoodKeeper App, FDA Food Safety Guidelines
  - Source: https://www.fda.gov/food/buy-store-serve-safe-food/safe-food-handling
  - Pasteurized milk typically lasts 5-7 days after opening when refrigerated at 40°F (4°C)

- **Optimal Temperature (4°C/40°F)**: FDA Food Code 3-501.16
  - Source: FDA Food Code 2017, Section 3-501.16 (Time/Temperature Control for Safety)
  - Cold holding temperature requirement: ≤41°F (5°C)

- **Temperature Sensitivity (2.0)**: Based on microbial growth rates
  - Source: Jay, J. M. (2000). Modern Food Microbiology (6th ed.)
  - Bacterial growth in milk doubles approximately every 20-30 minutes at room temperature

### Hard Cheese (Cheddar, Parmesan)
- **Shelf Life (180 days/6 months)**: USDA FoodKeeper App
  - Source: https://www.foodsafety.gov/keep-food-safe/foodkeeper-app
  - Unopened hard cheese: 6 months refrigerated
  - Opened: 3-4 weeks

- **Temperature Sensitivity (1.2)**: Lower than soft cheeses
  - Source: Fox, P. F. (2004). Cheese: Chemistry, Physics and Microbiology
  - Hard cheeses have lower water activity, more resistant to spoilage

### Yogurt
- **Shelf Life (21 days/3 weeks)**: USDA FoodKeeper, manufacturer guidelines
  - Source: USDA FoodKeeper App
  - Yogurt typically lasts 1-3 weeks past "best by" date when refrigerated

## MEAT & POULTRY

### Raw Chicken
- **Shelf Life (1-2 days)**: USDA Food Safety and Inspection Service (FSIS)
  - Source: https://www.fsis.usda.gov/food-safety/safe-food-handling-and-preparation/food-safety-basics/safe-temperature-chart
  - Raw poultry: 1-2 days in refrigerator

- **Optimal Temperature (2°C/35°F)**: USDA FSIS Guidelines
  - Source: USDA Refrigeration and Food Safety fact sheet
  - Coldest part of refrigerator (32-40°F / 0-4°C)

- **Temperature Abuse Threshold (2 hours)**: FDA "Danger Zone" Rule
  - Source: FDA Food Code, 2-hour/4-hour rule
  - Bacteria grow rapidly between 40-140°F (4-60°C)
  - Discard after 2 hours at room temperature

- **High Temperature Sensitivity (3.0)**: Microbial growth studies
  - Source: ICMSF (1996). Microorganisms in Foods 5: Characteristics of Microbial Pathogens
  - Salmonella and Campylobacter growth rates in poultry

### Raw Beef
- **Shelf Life (3-5 days)**: USDA FSIS
  - Source: https://www.fsis.usda.gov/food-safety/safe-food-handling-and-preparation/food-safety-basics/refrigeration
  - Steaks, roasts: 3-5 days
  - Ground beef: 1-2 days

### Ground Meat
- **Shelf Life (1-2 days)**: USDA FSIS
  - Source: Same as above
  - Higher surface area = faster spoilage than whole cuts
  - More susceptible to bacterial contamination

## PRODUCE

### Lettuce (Leafy Greens)
- **Shelf Life (7 days)**: USDA FoodKeeper
  - Source: USDA FoodKeeper App
  - Lettuce, spinach: 3-7 days when properly stored

- **Optimal Humidity (95%)**: Postharvest Technology Research Center, UC Davis
  - Source: Kader, A. A. (2002). Postharvest Technology of Horticultural Crops
  - Leafy greens require high humidity to prevent wilting

### Berries (Strawberries, Blueberries)
- **Shelf Life (5-7 days)**: USDA FoodKeeper
  - Source: USDA FoodKeeper App
  - Strawberries: 3-7 days
  - Blueberries: 1-2 weeks

- **Temperature Sensitivity**: Respiration rate studies
  - Source: Kader, A. A. (2002). Postharvest Technology of Horticultural Crops
  - Berries have high respiration rates, accelerate spoilage at higher temps

## EGGS

### Fresh Eggs
- **Shelf Life (3-5 weeks)**: USDA FSIS, FDA
  - Source: https://www.fsis.usda.gov/food-safety/safe-food-handling-and-preparation/eggs/shell-eggs-farm-table
  - Eggs can be stored 3-5 weeks in refrigerator
  - Often safe well past "best by" date

- **Optimal Temperature (4°C/40°F)**: FDA Food Code
  - Source: FDA Food Code 3-201.13
  - Shell eggs shall be stored at 45°F (7°C) or less

## LEFTOVERS

### Cooked Foods/Leftovers
- **Shelf Life (3-4 days)**: USDA FSIS
  - Source: https://www.fsis.usda.gov/food-safety/safe-food-handling-and-preparation/food-safety-basics/leftovers-and-food-safety
  - Cooked leftovers: 3-4 days refrigerated

- **2-Hour Rule**: FDA Food Code
  - Source: FDA Food Code 3-501.14
  - Discard perishable foods left at room temp > 2 hours (1 hour if > 90°F)

## TEMPERATURE ABUSE CALCULATIONS

### Arrhenius Equation Application
- **Q10 Rule (Temperature Coefficient)**: Food science standard
  - Source: van't Hoff, J. H. (1884). Études de dynamique chimique
  - Labuza, T. P. (1984). Application of chemical kinetics to deterioration of foods
  - Chemical reaction rates (including spoilage) approximately double for every 10°C increase

- **Activation Energy Values**: Food microbiology research
  - Source: Ratkowsky, D. A., et al. (1982). "Model for bacterial culture growth rate"
  - Ea values for bacterial growth: 50-100 kJ/mol

### Danger Zone
- **40-140°F (4-60°C)**: FDA Food Code
  - Source: FDA Food Code 3-501.16
  - Bacteria multiply rapidly in this range
  - Pathogenic bacteria can double every 20 minutes at optimal temps

## PACKAGING FACTORS

### Vacuum Sealing Benefits
- **Extended Shelf Life**: Food packaging research
  - Source: Robertson, G. L. (2012). Food Packaging: Principles and Practice (3rd ed.)
  - Vacuum packaging can extend shelf life 3-5x by reducing oxygen
  - Inhibits aerobic bacteria and oxidation

### Modified Atmosphere Packaging
- Source: Brody, A. L. (2005). Modified Atmosphere Food Packaging
  - Reducing oxygen slows microbial growth and oxidation

## CONSERVATIVE ESTIMATES

**Important Note**: The algorithm uses conservative estimates to err on the side of food safety:

- Shelf life values are on the lower end of recommended ranges
- Temperature abuse thresholds are stricter than minimum FDA requirements
- Safety categories transition earlier than absolute spoilage points
- "Caution" category provides buffer before actual unsafe conditions

This approach prioritizes user safety while minimizing food waste from premature disposal.

---

## NOTES ON VARIABILITY

Actual spoilage rates vary based on:
- Initial bacterial load
- Specific storage conditions
- Food handling practices
- Individual food quality
- Packaging integrity

The algorithm provides **estimates** based on typical conditions. Users should always use visual inspection, smell tests, and common sense in addition to these predictions.
